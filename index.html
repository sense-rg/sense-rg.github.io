Introduction, Context and Justification

Climate change ranks among the greatest challenges of the 21st century, while weather forecasting remains a problem of high mathematical and computational complexity. Atmospheric phenomena are complex dynamic systems whose understanding and modeling require knowledge from mathematics, physics and Earth sciences.

Improving systems for forecasting extreme events such as hurricanes, typhoons and monsoons can significantly strengthen early warning mechanisms, supporting authorities and civil defense agencies in implementing mitigation and adaptation measures. In this way, it becomes possible to reduce material damage, minimize socioeconomic impacts and preserve human lives.

Until the end of the 20th century, the modeling and forecasting of meteorological phenomena were predominantly based on statistical and probabilistic approaches, including Cox and Neyman–Scott processes, Gaussian random fields and fractal models. In recent decades, however, the advance of artificial intelligence and its growing application to environmental and meteorological monitoring have brought significant changes to this scenario.

Machine learning and deep learning techniques have been widely employed in tasks such as weather prediction, tracking convective systems and forecasting the trajectory and intensity of tropical cyclones. These methods have the advantage of integrating large volumes of heterogeneous data, providing robust results with high levels of accuracy.

In this context, satellite images, weather radar data and historical observation series play a fundamental role. Among the most promising techniques are Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), which have shown expressive results in applications such as reconstruction of weather radar images, tracking of tropical cyclones and real-time precipitation estimation.

The Northeast region of Brazil, especially the state of Ceará, is highly vulnerable to extreme hydrometeorological events, including severe droughts and localized flooding episodes. Such phenomena directly affect the population’s quality of life, compromise water security and produce negative impacts on several economic and social sectors.

History

This group is in part a formalization of the scientific cooperation established since 2020 between the Federal University of Ceará (UFC), the Ceará Foundation for Meteorology and Water Resources (FUNCEME) and the French Institut de Recherche pour le Développement (IRD), through the Géosciences Environnement Toulouse (GET) laboratory and the Observatoire Midi-Pyrénées (OMP). This partnership is coordinated by Prof. Dr. Tarcisio Ferreira Maciel (UFC) and Dr. Marielle Gosset (GET).

Within this collaboration, research has been developed focusing on different applications of artificial intelligence, remote sensing and signal processing in meteorology and hydrology. Among the main lines of investigation are:

  • Precipitation estimation from attenuation measurements in telecommunications radio links;
  • Forecasting satellite and weather radar images using Convolutional Long Short-Term Memory (ConvLSTM) neural networks;
  • Forecasting precipitation and solar radiation time series using Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) architectures;
  • Rainfall estimation from acoustic records;
  • Conversion between weather radar images and satellite images using deep learning techniques.

Over the last few years, the group has held scientific meetings under the RAINSMORE initiative, hosted in Fortaleza (2022), Quixadá (2023) and Toulouse (2024), promoting integration among researchers, students and partner institutions. As a result of this collaboration, some undergraduate monographs, master’s dissertations and doctoral theses have been produced and defended or are in development, in addition to publications in national and international conferences and in international scientific journals.

Currently, the group brings together undergraduate, master’s and doctoral students, as well as researchers linked to partner institutions, consolidating an international research network dedicated to the development of innovative methodologies for environmental monitoring, hydrometeorological forecasting and water resource management using artificial intelligence, remote sensing, and signal processing.

Team and Members

Prof. Tarcisio Ferreira Maciel, Dr.-Ing.

Bachelor’s (2002) and Master’s (2004) in Electrical Engineering from the Federal University of Ceará (UFC), with a one-year exchange in 1999 at Technische Universität Hamburg-Harburg (TUHH), Hamburg, Germany. PhD in Electrical Engineering and Information Technology at the Communications Engineering Lab, Technische Universität Darmstadt, Germany, in the area of wireless communications, radio resource management and smart antennas.

Since 2009, he has been a professor at UFC in the Department of Teleinformatics Engineering (DETI), also working in the Graduate Program in Teleinformatics Engineering (PPGETI), and researcher at the Wireless Telecommunications Research Group (GTEL), Applied Signal Processing Laboratory (LASP) and the National Institute of Science and Technology INCT Signals, developing research related to 5th and 6th generation telecommunications systems.

He has coordinated several international research and education projects (PROBRAL, CNPq-Internationalization, BRAFITEC, etc.). He is a senior member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), CNPq productivity fellow (level 1D), and reviewer and editor of international scientific journals. He has more than 180 publications, with over 2,500 citations, h-index 23 and i10-index 50. His areas of interest include optimization, wireless communications, signal and image processing, and machine learning.

Prof. Nícolas de Araújo Moreira, Dr.

Nícolas de Araújo Moreira is a tenured professor at the Federal University of Ceará (UFC) and researcher at the Computer Systems Engineering Laboratory (LESC), in the Department of Teleinformatics Engineering. He holds a degree in Electrical Engineering (2012) and a Master’s in Teleinformatics Engineering (2015) from UFC, a PhD in Electronics, Microelectronics, Optics and Lasers, Optoelectronics, Microwaves and Robotics from Université de Lille (France, 2019), and an MBA in Project and Process Management from IPOG (2022).

During his undergraduate studies, he spent part of his training at École Centrale de Nantes (France, 2010) through a European Union scholarship, interned at the Institut de Recherche en Électronique et Électrotechnique de Nantes-Atlantique (France, 2010) and at the National Institute for Space Research (2011–2012) through a call from the Brazilian Space Agency, and was an assistant professor at Institut Mines-Télécom Lille-Douai (2018–2019).

He currently teaches courses related to electronics and embedded systems. His interests include digital signal processing and artificial intelligence (AI), and since 2020 he has studied AI applications to meteorology in partnership with FUNCEME and IRD. He has worked on development projects with Siemens and DELL. He is a senior member of IEEE and reviewer of international scientific journals.
